Work and Life of Ila Sachani

Ila Sachani a young women was born in a farmer's family in the village of Moti Vavdi, Bhavnagar, which is in Gujarat. When Ila was born, the doctors told her parents that Ila would not be able to use her hands due to a disability. Growing up Ila had faced many challenges. She wanted to write, draw and do many other activities using her hands which she saw other children do. She wondered WHY COULDN'T SHE JOIN THEM??......

Her mother and grandmother who were skilled in the traditional art of Kathiawar embroidery were determined to encourage and teach Ila this art using her feet. Slowly and gradually Ila started getting better. Just imagine if people using their hands struggle to put a needle in the thread, how DIFFICULT would it be to do it by feet.....but Ila soon became a professional at it. With determination she learned many forms of embroidery even the ones outside Gujarat, like - Kachhi, Kashmiri, Lucknawi, and so on.

Now embroidery was a way to express herself for Ila. She first made fine designs just for her family, but then the people from her village came to know about Ila's work and were amazed. People started admiring and loving her art of embroidery and this soon began to spread. After a while, Ila's art reached the government office in Surat. They were also very impressed by her work and amazing story, so they decided to display some pieces of her work in a state exhibition. This was a complete new and different experience for Ila and opened new doors. Her talent soon spread across India. Her talent was no more hidden but she was famous!

By such a encouraging response of people Ila participated in more and more exhibitions and was given many awards. She became the celebrated artist and the receiver of the President's medal. Ila's story is an inspiration for all of us, on how a young girl born with physical disabilities became famous and admirable.
